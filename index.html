Child's Voice — What Your Child Thinks

These questions help us understand how your child sees themselves, their strengths, and what they want to achieve.

What does your child enjoy?
What activities, subjects or things make them happy?
What does your child find hard or dislike?
What activities or situations are difficult or frustrating for them?
How does your child feel about school?
Do they enjoy school? Any worries or concerns about school?
What about friendships and other children?
How does your child get on with other children? Do they have friends?
What is your child proud of?
What does your child see as their strengths or achievements?
What's the hardest thing (in your child's words)?
If your child had to explain their main difficulty, what would they say?
What help or support does your child want?
What would make things better for them? What help do they think they need?
What are your child's future hopes and dreams?
What does your child want to be or do when they grow up?
What or who is most important to your child?
What matters most to them? Who or what do they care about most?

Parent/Carer Views — Your Perspective

Please share your perspective as a parent or carer, based on your knowledge of your child at home and in the community.

What are your child's key strengths and positive qualities?
What are they good at? What makes them special or unique?
What are your main worries and concerns?
What are you most concerned about regarding your child's development?
When did you first notice concerns? How have things changed over time?
When did you first notice the difficulties? Have they improved, stayed the same, or got worse?
How do you feel your child gets on at school?
Your perception of school, and how the school feels about your child...
How do the difficulties affect your child at home?
How do these difficulties impact your child at home, family life, and routines?
What helps or works well at home?
What strategies, approaches, or situations help your child at home?
What experience have you had with professionals so far?
Have you seen doctors, therapists, teachers, or other professionals? What was helpful?
What are your hopes for your child in the next 12 months?
What would you like to see improve or change in the next year?
What are your longer-term hopes and aspirations for your child?
What do you hope for your child's future? What do you want for them long-term?
What would be important for the Educational Psychologist to know?
Anything else you think the EP should understand about your child or family?
How would you like to work with the school and professionals?
Any preferences for how support should be provided or how you'd like to be involved?
